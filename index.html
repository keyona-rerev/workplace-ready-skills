Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our workplace readiness programs.

What makes these programs different from traditional skills training?

+

Our programs use immersive simulations with real-world constraints and dynamic facilitation. Instead of lectures, students experience workplace scenarios with actual budget pressures, deadline demands, and professional standards‚Äîdeveloping skills through doing, not just hearing about them.

Do students need any prerequisites?

+

No prerequisites required. Programs are designed for students at grade-level literacy with no prior experience in research, business analysis, or professional writing. We build foundational competencies from the ground up.

How do you measure program success?

+

We track student engagement, skill progression across specific competencies, project completion rates, and quality of work products. Our admin dashboard provides real-time analytics and customizable reports for demonstrating program impact.

Can programs be customized for our school?

+

Yes! We adapt simulations to local Birmingham context, adjust time formats (intensive vs. after-school), and can integrate with existing curriculum. For Creative Coding, schools choose specific project focuses that align with their objectives.

Who delivers the instruction?

+

All programs are personally facilitated by our founder, Keyona Meeks, ensuring consistent quality and expertise. No teacher training required from your staff‚Äîwe handle all instruction and program delivery.

What's the ideal group size?

+

Intensive simulations work best with 15-30 students. After-school programs accommodate 12-20 students. Assemblies can serve entire grade levels or schools. We maintain these ranges to ensure quality facilitation and individual attention.

How quickly can we get started?

+

After initial consultation, we can typically launch programs within 2-4 weeks. Timeline depends on scheduling, format selected, and any customization requested. Contact us to discuss your specific timeline needs.
